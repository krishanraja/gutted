# Purpose

## Why gutted. exists

Your gut runs everything: digestion, immunity, mood, energy, sleep. And yet the moment something feels wrong, you're alone with a Notes app, a $400 lab report you can't read, and a generic diet that wasn't built for your body.

**gutted. exists to give every person an intelligent, personalised understanding of their gut -- and a clear daily path to improving it -- without needing a medical degree or a $500 specialist visit.**

---

## The problem in numbers

- **70M+ Americans** experience digestive disease (NIDDK).
- **40% of adults** report at least one functional GI disorder (Rome Foundation global study).
- The average gut-issue sufferer **waits 6+ months** before seeking help.
- The at-home gut testing market (Viome, GI-MAP, Thryve, Tiny Health, ZOE, etc.) crossed **$5B in 2024** and is growing >20% YoY -- but the *interpretation* gap is wider than ever.
- Most people **cannot read** their own lab results, and most doctors give them <15 minutes to discuss them.
- Generic diet advice fails because **every gut is different** -- and the data needed to personalise it (logs, biomarkers, restrictions) lives in five different apps that don't talk to each other.

The gap between *"something feels wrong"* and *"here's exactly what to do tomorrow"* is enormous, expensive, and emotionally exhausting.

## What we believe

1. **Tracking only works if it's frictionless.** Voice in 10 seconds beats typing for 2 minutes. Most users abandon trackers in 14 days because of friction, not because they stopped caring.
2. **Lab data is wasted unless it's interpreted in context.** A Viome score on its own is trivia. The same score, connected to your last 14 days of bloating logs, is a decision.
3. **Generic diets do harm.** A FODMAP plan for someone with celiac, or a low-fiber plan for someone with constipation, is worse than no plan.
4. **People deserve a single record of their gut.** Symptoms in one app, labs in a Drive folder, and meals on a Post-it is the actual problem.
5. **AI should never replace a doctor.** It should make every doctor visit more productive and every day in between more informed.

## Who we serve

People who:

- Live with recurring digestive discomfort -- bloating, IBS, GERD, food sensitivities, post-meal pain, irregularity.
- Have received a gut test (Viome, GI-MAP, SIBO, food sensitivity) they don't fully understand.
- Want food guidance that accounts for *their* body, not a Pinterest meal plan.
- Are proactive about wellness but exhausted by conflicting online advice.
- Value a modern, mobile-first, voice-first experience over clinical-feeling apps.

And, increasingly, the **practitioners** who treat them: nutritionists, functional-medicine clinicians, health coaches who need a way to keep their clients tracking between sessions and to get a clean read-only window into their data.

## What we explicitly are not

- **Not a medical device.** No diagnostic claims. The product is positioned and disclaimed as a tracking and insight tool.
- **Not a doctor replacement.** Every analysis includes "consult a healthcare professional" framing for serious symptoms; flagged logs surface the recommendation prominently.
- **Not a generic diet app.** Every recommendation is grounded in the user's logs, profile, and uploaded biomarkers.
- **Not a one-shot tool.** The value compounds with every log, upload, and chat. Day 30 is dramatically more useful than Day 1.

## The long-term vision

Become the **default record of personal gut health** -- the place where logs, lab reports, meal plans, and ongoing AI guidance converge. Over time gutted. is the authoritative timeline of each user's gut journey, and the bridge that makes specialist visits, employer wellness benefits, and at-home testing actually pay off.

## How we measure purpose

We're succeeding when:

- A user goes from *"something feels off"* to *"here is exactly what to eat this week"* in under 10 minutes.
- A user's average gut score improves over their first 30 days.
- A user shares their gutted. record with a practitioner instead of a one-line text message.
- Practitioners and at-home testing companies route their users to gutted. as the default interpretation layer.
- Free-to-paid conversion crosses 5%, paid churn stays under 8%, and at-home test partners hand us their distribution.
